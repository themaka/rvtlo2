What goes in each blank?
Condition
The circumstances under which learners will demonstrate the skill — what they'll have (“given a map”), what they'll be denied (“without a calculator”), or the setting or timing (“at the end of the semester”). It describes the performance, not the instruction.
Audience
Who the learners are: “students,” “third-grade students,” “new hires.”
Behavior
An observable, measurable action — what learners will do: label, calculate, assemble, critique. Avoid verbs you can't watch or check, like “understand” or “appreciate.”
Degree
How well they must perform for it to count: accuracy (“with 90% accuracy”), speed (“within five minutes”), or completeness (“all fifty states”).
